# CTA Playbook

## CTA Formula: Action Verb + Benefit

| Action Verb | + Benefit | = CTA |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Start | your free trial | Start your free trial |
| Get | the complete guide | Get the complete guide |
| Download | the SEO checklist | Download the SEO checklist |
| Join | 10,000+ marketers | Join 10,000+ marketers |
| Try | it free for 14 days | Try it free for 14 days |
| See | how it works | See how it works |
| Build | your first campaign | Build your first campaign |
| Discover | your SEO score | Discover your SEO score |

## Placement Rules

### Position 1: Within First 500 Words (Soft)
- After the introduction delivers initial value
- Low commitment: free resource, quick demo, template
- Feels like a natural extension, not an interruption
- Example: "Before we dive deeper, grab the worksheet we'll reference throughout: [Download free →]"

### Position 2: After Proof Section (Medium)
- Placed after case study, data, or comparison
- Medium commitment: free trial, audit, consultation
- Reader has seen evidence and is considering action
- Example: "These results aren't unique. [See what your site's SEO score looks like →]"

### Position 3: Conclusion (Strong)
- Final persuasion with full context
- Higher commitment: sign up, purchase, schedule call
- Include risk reversal: "No credit card required", "Cancel anytime"
- Example: "[Start optimizing your content today →] — Free 14-day trial, no credit card needed."

## Risk Reversal Phrases

Placed near CTAs to reduce friction:
- "No credit card required"
- "Cancel anytime"
- "Free for 14 days"
- "30-day money-back guarantee"
- "Set up in under 2 minutes"
- "No commitment, no contract"
- "Used by 10,000+ companies"
- "Rated 4.8/5 by customers"

## CTA by Content Type

| Content Type | Primary CTA | Secondary CTA |
|-------------|------------|---------------|
| Blog post (awareness) | Subscribe / Download guide | Read related post |
| Blog post (consideration) | Free trial / Demo | Case study |
| Comparison page | Free trial of recommended | Feature comparison deep dive |
| Landing page | Sign up / Buy | Watch demo video |
| Case study | Get a demo | Read more case studies |
| Email newsletter | Read full article | Reply with feedback |
| Social media | Link in bio / Comment | Save for later |

## A/B Testing Priority

Test these CTA elements in order of impact:
1. **Copy** (what the button says) — highest impact
2. **Placement** (where on the page) — high impact
3. **Context** (surrounding text) — medium impact
4. **Color/Design** (visual treatment) — lower impact than most assume
5. **Size** (button dimensions) — low impact unless currently invisible
